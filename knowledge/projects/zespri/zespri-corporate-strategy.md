# Zespri Corporate Strategy & Market Position

> **Source:** NZ Herald / YouTube interview with CEO Jason Te Brake (May 2026)
> **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=epGA5cj9p9g

## Financial Performance

- Global operating revenue increased by ~$1 billion
- Record harvest: 220.9 million trays in 2024/25 season
- Improved per-hectare productivity
- Strong returns delivered to growers despite global economic challenges
- Premium pricing maintained even in tough economic environments

## Supply Chain Strategy

- Deliberate growth of offshore production in Italy, France, and Greece
- 12-month supply strategy: fruit on shelves year-round to maintain brand presence and consumer loyalty
- Successfully navigated global supply chain disruptions while maintaining fruit quality and consistent retail supply
- Diversifies geographic risk

## Business Model

- Revenue from fruit sales + IP licensing + royalties
- IP and licensing contributes ~$213 million - a core pillar
- Primary growth driver is volume increases, not IP revenue
- Strong marketing investment solidifies premium brand positioning globally

## India Expansion

- New FTA described as a "huge unlock" by Te Brake
- Removes 33% tariff on kiwifruit into India
- Enables greater investment in marketing, infrastructure, and local capability
- Strategic priority market

## Investment Strategy

- Marketing investment to build brand demand
- Long-term partnerships
- Local talent development
- Infrastructure building in new markets

## Relevance to Our Work

**Digital Operations (Tim Lloyd, Head of Digital Operations):**
- ServiceNow Platform used as the "digital workflow layer" across ERP and operational platforms
- NOT replacing core business systems - focused on how people work
- Workflow Data Fabric connects and acts on data across systems in real time
- Focus: employee experience, execution efficiency, collaboration, task automation, issue resolution
- AI capabilities being adopted within ServiceNow context

**Implications for MCS:**
Zespri is growing aggressively. Their tech needs (MCS, supply chain systems) will scale with:
- More growers internationally (Italy, France, Greece supply)
- More markets (India)
- More complexity in clearance/maturity testing across geographies
- Potential future need for international MCS instances or multi-region support
